Remote Sensing: an educational resource.

The availability of open satellite imagery from around the world makes it easy to study the evolution of wildfires, floods, deforestation or urbanization.

Theoretical foundations about remote sensing are complex and not easily accessible for young people.

Therefor, the objective of Edusat is to present remote sensing to a non-specialized audience and offer a user-friendly tool for the analysis of land surface changes as well as a tool for the dissemination of results.

Three specific objectives stem from this main objective:

  • To design a teaching resource for educators in order to incorporate these competences in curricula.

The teaching material of EduSat is designed to be taught in two blocks:

a) Theoretical explanation of different consequences of global environmental change (biodiversity loss, climate change, land-use changes, pollution...), and what remote sensing is (types of satellites, sensors, band combinations, uses...)

b) Practical exercises on the identification of natural phenomena or anthropic processes, such as forest fires, floods, droughts, deforestation, urbanization, glacier regression, air pollution and volcanic eruptions, among many others.

Teaching Activity

How to use satellite imagery to identify the effects of global environmental change.


Duration of the activity

The workshop can take from a minimum of one hour to a maximum 15 hours, depending on the availability and the profile of the group.

Workshop Organization

  • Single-session workshops (maximum duration of 4 hours)
  • Multiple-session workshops

Multiple-session workshop example:

  • Session 1: Detailed explanation of satellites, sensors and their potential.
  • Session 2: Many and varied case studies are used to show the possibilities of remote sensing in identifying natural and anthropic events such as wildfires, floods, droughts, deforestation...
  • Session 3: Analysis in groups of students of a chosen natural or anthropic process.
  • Session 4: Presentation of the results of the different groups to their classmates.

Application cases

Several case studies have been chosen to graphically and visually show the consequences of different natural and anthropic events.

For each case study, the following is staded:

- The causes and consequences of the studied phenomenon.

- How Sentinel sensors can detect it: satellite types, indices, band combinations, ...

Conclusions

  • Far from being a discipline reserved for specialists and reduced to a select group of professionals, remote sensing is an affordable and highly educational tool.
  • Thanks to EU Copernicus program and some of the tools promoted around it, remote sensing is made accessible to students, educators and researchers.
  • The didactic and educational platform presents an objective and analytical vision regarding territorial processes resulting from global environmental change.

Conclusions

  • Far from being a discipline reserved for specialists and reduced to a select group of professionals, remote sensing is an affordable and highly educational tool.
  • Thanks to EU Copernicus program and some of the tools promoted around it, remote sensing is made accessible to students, educators and researchers.
  • The didactic and educational platform presents an objective and analytical vision regarding territorial processes resulting from global environmental change.
  • Through remote sensing, the ecological discourse emerging among young people is given empirical and experimental content.
